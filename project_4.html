Information Design

During this projects I went to work with multiple components of visual Information Design. The components were storytelling, motion design and infographics. The subject of this project was food and I got the idea to not make it about food in real life, but about the workings of food designed by others, which is food in videogames. This is my favorite project, because I chose a subject that fits my interest, but also because it didn't seem like it has been explored a lot before. There was a lot to research about it and I found a ton of insights. I put a lot of effort into the visuals and sprites of the animation. I especially take pride in the animation.

Other projects

  • Prepared

    Designing a travel app that takes different phases of a trip in consideration. Before, during and after the trip were the main focus during the design process of the app. After picking possible API's to use, I designed screenflows for both desktop and mobile.

  • Rest Quest

    A Ubiquitous Computing project, where I used persuasive patterns to design an Internet of Things solution, to lessen the computer usage of users after doing so for a long period of time. One of the most fun projects I've done.

Learning points

I tried a lot of new things during this project. For example, I never made pixel art sprites before this project. Then I put those sprites in a stop motion animation using Adobe Photoshop. It was difficult to keep the style consistent with the infographic and it took a bit of convincing the teachers, who eventually decided that the subject was very outside the box. I especially got into discovering visual styles because of this project and I hope to continue doing so.
